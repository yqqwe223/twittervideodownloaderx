❓ FAQs

Q: Is downloading Flickr videos legal?
A: Legality depends on copyright. This tool is for personal backup/education only. Please:
  • Only grab publicly accessible videos you’re allowed to view
  • Don’t use them commercially
  • Respect the original creator’s copyright
  • Follow Flickr’s Terms of Service
Q: Why can’t some videos be downloaded?
A: Likely reasons:
  • Video is private or password-protected
  • It’s been deleted or the link is dead
  • Your connection is unstable
  • Flickr is undergoing maintenance

Check the link, make sure you’re online, or try again later.

Q: Will the downloaded video have a watermark?
A: No. We fetch the original file straight from Flickr—no watermarks, no branding, identical quality.
Q: Do you support batch download?
A: Not yet. Grab links one at a time for now. Batch mode is on the roadmap—stay tuned!
Q: What format will the video be in?
A: Flickr usually serves:
  • MP4: most common, universal compatibility
  • MOV: some HD clips
  • FLV: older uploads

We pick the best container automatically so the file plays everywhere.

Q: Download speed is slow—any fix?
A: Speed depends on:
  • Your own bandwidth
  • Flickr server load
  • File size
  • Concurrent downloads

Pick a lower quality or retry when your network is quieter.
